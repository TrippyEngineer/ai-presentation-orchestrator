Country Team Meeting (Sample)
Date: [Sample Date]
Attendees: Alex (facilitator), Dr. Amara Diallo (Country Lead),
           James Okonkwo (M&E Manager), Fatima Hassan (Programme Officer),

Alex: Okay let's get started. We have about 45 minutes.
Main agenda is the Q1 review, the supply chain issue that
came up last week, and the donor report deadline. Amara,
do you want to kick off?

Dr. Amara Diallo: Yes. So Q1 coverage numbers are actually
better than we expected. We hit 73% for the SMC distribution
in Kisumu and Nakuru. The challenge is Mombasa — we're at
51% and that's because of the CHW training backlog. James,
you want to speak to that?

James Okonkwo: Right so we have 34 CHWs who haven't completed
the updated protocol training. It was supposed to happen in
January but the training venue fell through. We've rebooked
for March 8th but that's going to push our Q1 close numbers.
I think we need to flag this to the donor.

Fatima Hassan: I agree we need to flag it but I want to make
sure we frame it right. It's not a programme failure, it's a
logistics issue that we've already resolved. Can we get that
language into the donor report clearly?

Dr. Sarah Kimani: The other thing we need to discuss is the
cold chain issue in Nakuru. Three refrigeration units failed
in the last two weeks. I've been in contact with the supplier
but I don't have a confirmed replacement date yet. This could
affect Q2 distribution.

Alex: Okay so we have a few action items emerging. James,
can you own the CHW training completion and make sure March 8th
is confirmed? And can you give me a number by Friday — how
many CHWs will be trained by Q1 close?

James Okonkwo: Yes I can do that. I'll send you the confirmed
number by end of day Friday.

Alex: Sarah, the cold chain issue — who is the supplier
contact and when can we expect a resolution?

Dr. Sarah Kimani: The supplier is MedEquip Ltd. I've spoken
to their logistics manager but they haven't given me a date.
I think we need to escalate — maybe Amara can make a direct
call to their country director.

Dr. Amara Diallo: I can do that. Let me try to reach him this
week. But I want to be clear — if we don't have replacement
units by March 15th we are looking at a real Q2 risk.

Alex: Noted. Amara, please make that call and let us know
by Wednesday what the timeline looks like.

Fatima Hassan: On the donor report — the deadline is March 5th.
I've started drafting but I need the final Q1 coverage numbers
from James and the cold chain status from Sarah before I can
finish the risk section. When can I get those?

James Okonkwo: I'll have the numbers by Friday as I said.

Dr. Sarah Kimani: I should have clarity on cold chain by
Wednesday after Amara speaks to the supplier.

Fatima Hassan: Okay so realistically I can finish the draft
by March 3rd if I have both by Wednesday-Friday. That gives
us two days to review before the deadline.

Alex: That's tight but workable. Let's put March 3rd as
internal draft deadline. Everyone should review by March 4th,
submit to donor March 5th.

Dr. Amara Diallo: One more thing — there's an internal
review scheduled for March 10th with headquarters. I haven't

Alex: I'll take that — I'll reach out to headquarters
today and find out who the lead is and get the agenda.

Alex: Okay let's close. Summary of what I heard: James

Fatima is drafting the donor report, needs both inputs.
Internal draft by March 3rd, submit March 5th. I'll follow
